# Alternative Research Architectures

## Alternative A: Evidence-First Architecture

**Core Principle:** Start with evidence, derive everything else.

**Flow:**

```
Evidence → Observation → Question → Investigation → Finding → Knowledge
```

**Advantages:**

- Ensures evidence exists before conclusions
- Prevents speculation
- Grounds research in reality

**Disadvantages:**

- May miss important questions
- May be too slow for exploratory research
- May not capture all evidence

---

## Alternative B: Question-First Architecture

**Core Principle:** Start with questions, investigate to answer them.

**Flow:**

```
Question → Investigation → Evidence → Finding → Knowledge
```

**Advantages:**

- Clear direction
- Focused research
- Measurable progress

**Disadvantages:**

- May miss important evidence
- May be too narrow
- May not capture unexpected findings

---

## Alternative C: Ontology-First Architecture

**Core Principle:** Start with ontological understanding, derive everything else.

**Flow:**

```
Ontology → Question → Investigation → Evidence → Finding → Knowledge
```

**Advantages:**

- Ensures ontological clarity
- Prevents category errors
- Grounds research in reality

**Disadvantages:**

- May be too abstract
- May miss practical concerns
- May be slow to produce results

---

## Alternative D: Model-First Architecture

**Core Principle:** Start with models, test against evidence.

**Flow:**

```
Model → Evidence → Question → Investigation → Finding → Knowledge
```

**Advantages:**

- Ensures models are testable
- Prevents untestable theories
- Grounds models in evidence

**Disadvantages:**

- May miss important questions
- May be biased by initial model
- May not capture all evidence

---

## Alternative E: Knowledge-First Architecture

**Core Principle:** Start with existing knowledge, identify gaps.

**Flow:**

```
Knowledge → Gap → Question → Investigation → Finding → Knowledge
```

**Advantages:**

- Builds on existing knowledge
- Identifies gaps systematically
- Prevents duplication

**Disadvantages:**

- May be biased by existing knowledge
- May miss new paradigms
- May be too conservative

---

## Alternative F: Audit-First Architecture

**Core Principle:** Start with audit, identify what needs investigation.

**Flow:**

```
Audit → Gap → Question → Investigation → Finding → Knowledge
```

**Advantages:**

- Ensures completeness
- Identifies blind spots
- Prevents missed areas

**Disadvantages:**

- May be too slow
- May be too thorough
- May not prioritize correctly
