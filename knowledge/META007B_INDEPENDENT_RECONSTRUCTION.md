## Starting Point

**What is architectural thinking?**

**Definition:** The cognitive process by which architectural understanding is produced, validated, and preserved.

## Reconstruction from First Principles

### Step 1: What Must Happen for Thinking to Occur?

**Observation 1:** Thinking requires a MIND (or system capable of cognition).

**Observation 2:** Thinking requires INPUT (something to think about).

**Observation 3:** Thinking requires OUTPUT (something produced by thinking).

**Observation 4:** Thinking requires PROCESS (mechanism of thinking).

---

### Step 2: What Are the Minimal Processes?

**Process 1:** ATTENTION — focusing on something.

**Process 2:** PERCEPTION — becoming aware of something.

**Process 3:** DIFFERENTIATION — distinguishing something from something else.

**Process 4:** EVALUATION — assessing something.

**Process 5:** INTEGRATION — combining elements into coherent whole.

---

### Step 3: Are These Minimal?

**Can ATTENTION be decomposed?**

- ATTENTION requires SELECTING what to focus on
- SELECTING requires COMPARING alternatives
- But COMPARING requires ATTENTION
- Circular dependency

**Verdict:** ATTENTION may not be primitive — it depends on SELECTING.

---

**Can PERCEPTION be decomposed?**

- PERCEPTION requires SENSING (receiving input)
- PERCEPTION requires INTERPRETING (making sense of input)
- But INTERPRETING requires PERCEPTION

**Verdict:** PERCEPTION may not be primitive — it depends on INTERPRETING.

---

**Can DIFFERENTIATION be decomposed?**

- DIFFERENTIATION requires COMPARING two things
- COMPARING requires OBSERVING both things
- But OBSERVING requires ATTENTION

**Verdict:** DIFFERENTIATION depends on OBSERVING and ATTENTION.

---

**Can EVALUATION be decomposed?**

- EVALUATION requires COMPARING to criteria
- EVALUATION requires JUDGING what is better/worse
- But JUDGING requires EVALUATION

**Verdict:** EVALUATION may not be primitive — it depends on JUDGING.

---

**Can INTEGRATION be decomposed?**

- INTEGRATION requires COMBINING parts
- COMBINING requires RECOGNIZING parts
- RECOGNIZING requires OBSERVING

**Verdict:** INTEGRATION depends on RECOGNIZING and OBSERVING.
