# Thinking Lifecycle

## Lifecycle States

| # | State | Description | Duration |
|---|-------|-------------|----------|
| 0 | UNKNOWN | No thinking has occurred | Until attention is focused |
| 1 | ATTENDING | Attention is focused on something | Until observation occurs |
| 2 | OBSERVING | Something has been noticed | Until comparison occurs |
| 3 | COMPARING | Two things are being related | Until judgment occurs |
| 4 | JUDGING | Something is being evaluated | Until interpretation occurs |
| 5 | INTERPRETING | Meaning is being understood | Until abstraction occurs |
| 6 | ABSTRACTING | Essence is being extracted | Until model is formed |
| 7 | MODELED | Conceptual representation exists | Until verification occurs |
| 8 | VERIFIED | Model has been tested | Until knowledge is produced |
| 9 | KNOWLEDGE | Verified understanding exists | Until decision occurs |
| 10 | DECIDING | Choice is being made | Until memory occurs |
| 11 | REMEMBERED | Knowledge is preserved | Permanent |

# Key Insight

**Thinking has a lifecycle from UNKNOWN to REMEMBERED.**

**The lifecycle includes feedback loops — knowledge can trigger new attention.**

**Thinking is RECURSIVE — it can think about itself.**
