# 2. Hidden Assumptions

## 2.1 ASSUMPTION-001: Single Parser Architecture

### Assumption

SvitloSk has a SINGLE Parser component.

### Evidence

> "Parser: A software Component responsible for retrieving Open Data from approved Data Sources." (GLOSSARY.md §2)

> "Parser is the canonical architectural Component responsible for retrieving Open Data." (GLOSSARY.md §7)

### Challenge

Architect Intent specifies TWO independent parsers.

### Status: HIDDEN

This assumption is not explicitly stated but influences the architecture.

---

## 2.2 ASSUMPTION-002: Telegram as Primary Channel

### Assumption

Telegram is the PRIMARY publication channel.

### Evidence

> "Telegram Journal: The official public communication channel operated by the SvitloSk Project." (GLOSSARY.md §3)

> "Telegram Journal Specifications (TJS)" (GLOSSARY.md §1)

### Challenge

Architect Intent states Telegram and Facebook are PEER adapters, neither is primary.

### Status: HIDDEN

This assumption is not explicitly stated but influences the architecture.

---

## 2.3 ASSUMPTION-003: Journal as Primary Channel

### Challenge

This assumption is not explicitly stated but influences the architecture.

## 2.6 ASSUMPTION-006: Domain Merging

### Assumption

All electricity information can be merged into a single model.

### Evidence

> "Planned Power Outages originate exclusively from an approved Data Source." (GLOSSARY.md §5)

> "Emergency Power Outages SHALL be treated as authoritative immediately after publication." (GLOSSARY.md §5)

### Challenge

Architect Intent explicitly states: "Queue Schedule, Planned Outages, Emergency Outages are THREE DIFFERENT ONTOLOGICAL OBJECTS. Never merge them."

### Status: HIDDEN

This assumption is not explicitly stated but influences the data model.

## 2.7 ASSUMPTION-007: Journal Contains Queue Information

### Assumption

Journal contains Queue Schedule information.

### Evidence

> "One journal edition may contain: planned outages (today), emergency outages (today), planned outages (tomorrow), emergency outages (tomorrow), tomorrow queue graph..." (Architect Intent)

### Challenge

Queue Schedule is primarily served by PWA, not Journal.

Journal may only contain the "tomorrow queue graph," not the full Queue Schedule.

### Status: HIDDEN

This assumption is not explicitly stated but influences the Journal scope.

---

## 2.10 ASSUMPTION-010: Channel Independence

### Assumption

All channels receive the same information.

### Evidence

> "A Publication MAY be distributed through multiple Publication Channels." (GLOSSARY.md §3)

### Challenge

PWA receives Queue Schedule directly.

Journal receives Outage information via Telegram/Facebook.

These are different information flows.

### Status: HIDDEN

This assumption is not explicitly stated but influences the publishing architecture.

# 3. Traditional Publishing Inheritance

## 3.1 INHERITANCE-001: Single Newspaper Analogy

### Inherited Concept

SvitloSk is like a NEWSPAPER with a single publication flow.

### Challenge

SvitloSk has THREE independent information flows:
- Queue Schedule → PWA
- Planned Outages → Journal
- Emergency Outages → Journal

### Status: POTENTIALLY INVALID

The single newspaper analogy does not apply.

---

## 3.2 INHERITANCE-002: Telegram as Newspaper Front Page

### Inherited Concept

Telegram Journal is the "front page" of SvitloSk.

### Challenge

Architect Intent states Telegram is just one adapter among peers.

PWA is equally important for Queue Schedule.

### Status: POTENTIALLY INVALID

The front page analogy does not apply.

---

## 3.3 INHERITANCE-003: Publication as Universal Currency

### Inherited Concept

Publication is the universal unit of information.

### Challenge

Queue Schedule uses Push Notifications, not Publications.

PWA displays Timelines and Graphs, not Publications.

### Status: POTENTIALLY INVALID

Publication is not universal across all domains.
