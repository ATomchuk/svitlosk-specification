# 2. Candidate Glossary

## 2.1 Core Concepts

### Information Product

A discrete unit of information produced by SvitloSk for distribution to residents.

**Source:** This investigation.

---

### Journal Edition

A container for all Information Products for one day.

**Source:** Architect Intent, CASE-JRN-001.

---

### Product Type

The category of Information Product (Emergency, Planned, Queue, Technical, Service).

**Source:** This investigation.

---

### Product Identity

The unique identifier for an Information Product.

**Source:** This investigation.

---

## 2.2 Product Types

### Emergency Outage Publication

An Information Product containing emergency outage information for one Territory.

**Source:** Architect Intent, GLOSSARY §5.

---

### Planned Outage Publication (Today)

An Information Product containing today's planned outage information for one Territory.

**Source:** Architect Intent, GLOSSARY §5.

---

### Planned Outage Publication (Tomorrow)

A TEMPORARY Information Product containing tomorrow's planned outage information for one Territory.

**Source:** Architect Intent, GLOSSARY §3.

---

### Queue Graph Publication (Tomorrow)

A COMPOSITE Information Product containing tomorrow's queue schedule visualization.

**Source:** Architect Intent.

---

### Technical Publication

An Information Product containing system updates and technical information.

**Source:** Architect Intent.

---

### Service Publication (Future)

An Information Product containing future project announcements.

**Source:** Architect Intent.

---

## 2.3 Product Dimensions

### Domain Dimension

The information domain the product belongs to (Queue, Planned, Emergency, System).

**Source:** Architect Intent.

---

### Temporal Dimension

The time scope of the product (Today, Tomorrow, System-wide).

**Source:** This investigation.

---

### Persistence Dimension

Whether the product is permanent or temporary.

**Source:** This investigation.

---

### Organization Dimension

How the product is organized (Territory-based, Queue-based, System-wide).

**Source:** This investigation.
