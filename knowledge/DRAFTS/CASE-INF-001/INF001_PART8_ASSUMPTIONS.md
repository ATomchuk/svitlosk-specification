# 1. Purpose

This document identifies hidden assumptions, especially those inherited from newspapers, social media, CMS systems, and publishing systems.

# 2. Hidden Assumptions

## 2.1 ASSUMPTION-001: Single Information Flow

### Assumption

All information flows through a single pipeline.

### Evidence

> "Processing Cycle: One complete execution of the information pipeline." (GLOSSARY §2)

### Challenge

Architect Intent specifies THREE independent information domains with DIFFERENT flows.

Queue Schedule → PWA.

Planned/Emergency Outages → Journal.

### Source: Publishing Systems

Traditional publishing systems have a single editorial pipeline.

SvitloSk has multiple independent flows.

### Status: HIDDEN

---

## 2.2 ASSUMPTION-002: Publication as Universal Currency

### Assumption

All information is distributed as Publications.

### Evidence

> "Publication: A public information message generated by the SvitloSk system for distribution through one or more supported communication channels." (GLOSSARY §3)

### Challenge

Queue Schedule is distributed via PWA Timeline, not as Publications.

Push Notifications are not Publications.

### Source: Publishing Systems

Traditional publishing systems distribute everything as "publications."

SvitloSk has multiple distribution mechanisms.

### Status: HIDDEN

---

## 2.3 ASSUMPTION-003: Channel Ownership

### Assumption

Channels own the information they distribute.

### Evidence

> "Telegram Journal: The official public communication channel operated by the SvitloSk Project." (GLOSSARY §3)

### Challenge

Architect Intent explicitly states: "Telegram and Facebook DO NOT own information. They receive prepared representations."

### Source: Social Media

Social media platforms "own" the content posted to them.

SvitloSk channels are just representations.

### Status: HIDDEN

---

## 2.4 ASSUMPTION-004: Single Data Model

### Assumption

All information shares a single data model.

### Evidence

> "The logical data model is the canonical representation of information used throughout the SvitloSk Project." (DATA_MODEL.md)

### Challenge

Architect Intent specifies THREE independent domains with DIFFERENT structures.

Queue-based (Domain A) vs Address-based (Domains B, C).

### Source: CMS Systems

CMS systems have a single content model.

SvitloSk has multiple domain-specific models.

### Status: HIDDEN

---

## 2.5 ASSUMPTION-005: Journal as Primary

### Assumption

Journal is the primary information product.

### Evidence

> "The SvitloSk Public Information Journal is the primary public communication channel of the project." (CHARTER §10)

### Challenge

PWA is equally important for Queue Schedule.

Journal is ONE product, not the ONLY product.

### Source: Newspapers

Newspapers have a single primary product (the newspaper).

SvitloSk has multiple products (Journal, PWA).

### Status: HIDDEN

---

## 2.6 ASSUMPTION-006: Address as Universal Identifier

### Assumption

All information can be identified by address.

### Evidence

> "Address: A geographical location published by the official Data Source." (GLOSSARY §4)

### Challenge

Queue Schedule is NOT address-based.

It uses Queue/Subqueue identity, not Address identity.

### Source: CMS Systems

CMS systems often use address/URL as universal identifier.

SvitloSk has multiple identity dimensions.

### Status: HIDDEN

---

## 2.7 ASSUMPTION-007: Daily as Universal Period

### Assumption

All information is organized by daily periods.

### Evidence

> "One journal edition may include: planned outages (today), emergency outages (today)..." (Architect Intent)

### Challenge

Queue Schedule changes HOURLY, not daily.

Planned Outages have specific time intervals, not just daily.

### Source: Newspapers

Newspapers are organized by daily editions.

SvitloSk has hourly and interval-based information.

### Status: HIDDEN

---

## 2.8 ASSUMPTION-008: Push Notifications as Universal

### Assumption

Push notifications can be used for all information types.

### Evidence

> "Notification Services" (ARCHITECTURE §Layer 8)

### Challenge

Architect Intent explicitly states: "Push notifications belong ONLY to Queue Schedule."

### Source: Social Media

Social media uses notifications for all content types.

SvitloSk restricts notifications to Queue Schedule only.

### Status: HIDDEN

---

## 2.9 ASSUMPTION-009: Information Equals Representation

### Assumption

Information and its representation are the same thing.

### Evidence

> "Telegram Journal: The official public communication channel..." (GLOSSARY §3)

### Challenge

Architect Intent explicitly separates information from representation.

"Telegram and Facebook DO NOT own information."

### Source: Social Media

On social media, the post IS the information.

In SvitloSk, the post is just a REPRESENTATION of information.

### Status: HIDDEN

---

## 2.10 ASSUMPTION-010: Single Source of Truth

### Assumption

There is a single source of truth for all information.

### Evidence

> "Official Open Data SHALL remain the only operational source of truth." (DATA_MODEL.md)

### Challenge

This is CORRECT for information origin.

But SvitloSk also creates KNOWLEDGE through interpretation.

Knowledge is a SEPARATE layer from information.

### Source: CMS Systems

CMS systems often conflate data and information.

SvitloSk separates information (from DSO) from knowledge (from interpretation).

### Status: PARTIALLY HIDDEN

# 3. Traditional Publishing Inheritance

## 3.1 INHERITANCE-001: Single Newspaper Analogy

### Inherited Concept

SvitloSk is like a NEWSPAPER with a single publication flow.

### Challenge

SvitloSk has THREE independent information flows:
- Queue Schedule → PWA
- Planned Outages → Journal
- Emergency Outages → Journal

### Status: POTENTIALLY INVALID

---

## 3.2 INHERITANCE-002: Editorial Board

### Inherited Concept

SvitloSk has an editorial board making content decisions.

### Challenge

SvitloSk is AUTOMATED.

It does not have editorial decisions — it interprets official data.

### Status: POTENTIALLY INVALID

---

## 3.3 INHERITANCE-003: Subscriber Model

### Inherited Concept

Residents are "subscribers" who receive all content.

### Challenge

Residents CONFIGURE what they receive.

Push notifications are OPTIONAL and CONFIGURABLE.

### Status: POTENTIALLY INVALID

---

## 3.4 INHERITANCE-004: Content Management System

### Inherited Concept

SvitloSk is a CMS that manages content.

### Challenge

SvitloSk does not CREATE content.

It INTERPRETS official data and creates REPRESENTATIONS.

### Status: POTENTIALLY INVALID

---

## 3.5 INHERITANCE-005: Social Media Platform

### Inherited Concept

SvitloSk is a social media platform with posts and followers.

### Challenge

SvitloSk channels are just REPRESENTATIONS.

The information exists independently of any channel.

### Status: POTENTIALLY INVALID
