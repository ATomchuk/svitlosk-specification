# 2. Hidden Assumptions

## 2.1 ASSUMPTION-001: Journal is a Publication Medium

### Assumption

Journal is a MEDIUM through which Publications are delivered (like a newspaper, magazine, or TV channel).

### Evidence

> "The SvitloSk Public Information Journal is the primary public communication channel of the project." (CHARTER.md §10)

### Analysis

The word "channel" in CHARTER.md §10 implies Journal is a MEDIUM.

But a medium is a DELIVERY MECHANISM, not the CONTENT itself.

If Journal is a medium, then what is the content?

### Challenge

Is Journal the MEDIUM or the CONTENT?

If Journal is the medium, then Publications are the content.

If Journal is the content, then what is the medium?

### Status: HIDDEN

This assumption is not explicitly stated but influences the ontology.

---

## 2.2 ASSUMPTION-002: Publication is the Atomic Unit

### Assumption

Publication is the SMALLEST INDIVISIBLE unit of information in the system.

### Evidence

### Analysis

The Glossary defines Publication as a "message."

But is Publication truly atomic?

Could a Publication be decomposed into smaller units (e.g., paragraphs, sentences, data points)?

### Challenge

Is Publication truly atomic, or is it a COMPOSITE of smaller units?

This assumption is not explicitly stated but influences the ontology.

# 3. Traditional Publishing Inheritance

## 3.1 INHERITANCE-001: Newspaper Analogy

### Inherited Concept

Journal is like a NEWSPAPER — it has daily editions (issues), articles (publications), and sections (territories).

### Source

Traditional publishing terminology (newspaper, magazine, journal).

### Challenge

Is this analogy valid for an information service?

Newspapers have EDITORS who make editorial decisions.

SvitloSk has AUTOMATED SYSTEMS that generate Publications without editorial intervention.

### Status: POTENTIALLY INVALID

The newspaper analogy may not apply to an automated information service.

---

## 3.2 INHERITANCE-002: Broadcast Analogy

### Inherited Concept

Journal is like a BROADCAST CHANNEL — it continuously transmits information to subscribers.

### Source

Traditional broadcasting terminology (TV channel, radio station).

### Challenge

Is this analogy valid for an information service?

Broadcasts are CONTINUOUS and REAL-TIME.

SvitloSk Publications are DISCRETE and PERIODIC.

### Status: POTENTIALLY INVALID

The broadcast analogy may not apply to a periodic information service.

---

## 3.3 INHERITANCE-003: Archive Analogy

### Inherited Concept

Journal is like an ARCHIVE — it stores historical information for future reference.

### Source

Traditional archiving terminology (library, archive, repository).

### Challenge

Is this analogy valid for an information service?

Archives are PASSIVE — they store information but do not distribute it.

SvitloSk is ACTIVE — it analyzes, interprets, and distributes information.

### Status: POTENTIALLY INVALID

The archive analogy may not capture the active nature of SvitloSk.

---

## 3.4 INHERITANCE-004: Issue as Editorial Concept

### Inherited Concept

Issue is an EDITORIAL concept — it represents one edition of a publication.

### Source

Traditional publishing terminology (magazine issue, newspaper edition).

### Challenge

Is this concept valid for an automated system?

Issues in traditional publishing involve EDITORIAL DECISIONS.

SvitloSk has MINIMAL EDITORIAL INTERVENTION — Publications are generated automatically.

### Status: POTENTIALLY INVALID

The editorial concept of Issue may not apply to an automated system.

---

## 3.5 INHERITANCE-005: Release as Versioning Concept

### Inherited Concept

Release is a VERSIONING concept — it represents one version of a product.

### Source

Software engineering terminology (release, version, deployment).

### Challenge

Is this concept valid for a publication?

Releases in software engineering involve CHANGES and UPDATES.

SvitloSk Publications are STATIC once generated (they do not change).

### Status: POTENTIALLY INVALID

The versioning concept of Release may not apply to static Publications.
