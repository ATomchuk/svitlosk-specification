# 4. Key Findings

## 4.1 Journal Ontological Category

**Journal is a POLYMORPHIC concept that cannot be reduced to a single ontological category.**

It is simultaneously a Service, Channel, and Archive.

**Evidence:** CHARTER.md §1, §10.

**Confidence:** HIGH.

## 4.2 Publication as Atomic Unit

**Publication is the ATOMIC OBJECT — the smallest indivisible unit.**

It is the only concept that can be directly distributed to residents.

**Evidence:** GLOSSARY.md §3.

**Confidence:** HIGH.

## 4.3 Identity Dimensions

**Reporting Period and Territory are IDENTITY DIMENSIONS, not objects.**

They identify WHEN and WHERE Publications belong, but are not Publications themselves.

**Evidence:** GLOSSARY.md §2, §4.

**Confidence:** HIGH.

## 4.4 Issue as Temporal Instantiation

**Issue is a TEMPORAL INSTANTIATION — the daily instance of the Journal.**

It opens when the first Publication is generated and represents one editorial edition.

**Evidence:** TELEGRAM_PUBLICATION_LIFECYCLE.md.

**Confidence:** HIGH.

## 4.5 Release Uncertainty

**Release is a COMPOSITE ENTITY, but its existence is uncertain.**

It appears only in user context, not in canonical documents.

**Evidence:** User context only.

**Confidence:** MEDIUM.

## 4.6 Undefined Concepts

**Entry, Notice, and Information Object are NOT concepts in the current project ontology.**

They have no evidence in canonical documents.

**Evidence:** Analysis of GLOSSARY.md, CHARTER.md.

**Confidence:** HIGH.

## 4.7 Contradictions

**Eight contradictions were identified.**

Three are high severity, three are medium severity, two are low severity.

The highest severity contradictions relate to the ontological category of Journal.

**Evidence:** Analysis of CHARTER.md, GLOSSARY.md, TELEGRAM_PUBLICATION_LIFECYCLE.md.

**Confidence:** HIGH.

## 4.8 Hidden Assumptions

**Ten hidden assumptions were identified.**

The most significant is that Journal is a medium.

**Evidence:** Analysis of CHARTER.md, GLOSSARY.md.

**Confidence:** HIGH.

## 4.9 Traditional Publishing Inheritance

**Five traditional publishing inheritances were identified.**

These inheritances may not apply to an automated information service.

**Evidence:** CHARTER.md §1, §7.

**Confidence:** HIGH.

## 4.10 Competing Models

**Six competing ontological models were constructed.**

Model C (Journal as Territory-Organized Structure) is most semantically complete.

**Evidence:** Analysis of CHARTER.md, GLOSSARY.md, User context.

**Confidence:** HIGH.
