# DRAFT-P-002_Multiple_Models_Before_Recommendation

**Document ID:** DRAFT-P-002

**Title:** Multiple Models Before Recommendation

**Status:** Draft

---

# Statement

Construct multiple competing models before choosing one.

---

# Reasoning

This principle emerged from the investigation methodology. Every investigation constructed multiple models:

- CASE-001A: 11 competing models for Publication
- CASE-002A: 7 competing models for Issue
- META-001: 20 ontological types identified
- CASE-INF-001: 7 Information models

Multiple models reveal hidden assumptions, identify contradictions, and show that no single model was sufficient.

---

# Evidence

- CASE-001A: 11 models for Publication
- CASE-002A: 7 models for Issue
- META-001: 20 ontological types
- CASE-INF-001: 7 Information models
- All models were cross-examined before any conclusions

---

# Source

Investigation methodology — emerged from practice, not design.

---

# Applications

- Every future investigation should construct multiple models
- Models should be cross-examined before conclusions
- No single model should be assumed correct

---

# Related Concepts

- Research Before Change
- Architect Decides, CLI Researches
