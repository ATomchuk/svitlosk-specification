# Recommended Editorial Principles

After review, deduplication, and analysis, the following 10 editorial principles are recommended for the Telegram Editorial Model:

---

## EP-001: Reader First Principle (RECOMMENDED)

**Statement:** The primary audience is the residents of the Starokostiantyniv Territorial Community. All editorial decisions shall prioritize the informational needs of this community.

**Source:** TJS-001 §8

**Classification:** Editorial Principle

**Status:** RETAINED (absorbs EP-012 Community-First)

---

## EP-002: Editorial Truth Principle (RECOMMENDED)

**Statement:** Every publication must increase clarity without changing the meaning of the official source. The journal does not interpret, predict or alter official information. No assumptions, forecasts or predictions SHALL be presented as facts.

**Source:** TJS-001 §6, PP P-03

**Classification:** Editorial Principle

**Status:** RETAINED (absorbs EP-006 Source Fidelity)

---

## EP-003: Editorial Silence Principle (RECOMMENDED)

**Statement:** SvitloSk does not modify open data. SvitloSk does not create new facts. SvitloSk does not produce forecasts. SvitloSk does not alter the meaning of officially published information.

**Source:** CHARTER §7

**Classification:** Editorial Principle

**Status:** RETAINED

---

## EP-004: Minimal Editorial Intervention Principle (RECOMMENDED)

**Statement:** Every publication is generated automatically from structured data. Manual editing is not part of the standard publication workflow. After publication: factual corrections are permitted; layout corrections are permitted; the meaning of official information shall never change.

**Source:** TJS-004 §Automatic, TJS-003 §10

**Classification:** Editorial Principle

**Status:** RETAINED

---

## EP-005: Issue Integrity Principle (RECOMMENDED)

**Statement:** The Telegram Journal serves as the permanent public archive of publications related to the current day. Daily publications concerning planned and emergency outages remain available as part of the historical record.

**Source:** TJS-001 §11

**Classification:** Editorial Principle

**Status:** RETAINED

---

## EP-007: Deterministic Editorial Behaviour Principle (RECOMMENDED)

**Statement:** Two identical normalized Datasets SHALL always generate identical Telegram Journals. Publication order, formatting and content SHALL remain deterministic.

**Source:** TJS-008 §9

**Classification:** Editorial Principle

**Status:** RETAINED

---

## EP-009: Territory-First Principle (RECOMMENDED)

**Statement:** Territory name is always the first element of the publication. A post represents only one territory.

**Source:** TJS-004 §Territory

**Classification:** Editorial Principle

**Status:** RETAINED

---

## EP-010: Consistency Principle (RECOMMENDED)

**Statement:** The objective is to ensure that every publication is consistent, easy to read, predictable and automatically generated from structured data.

**Source:** TJS-004 §Purpose

**Classification:** Editorial Principle

**Status:** RETAINED

---

## EP-013: Accessibility Principle (RECOMMENDED)

**Statement:** Publications SHALL be accessible to all residents regardless of technical experience or device.

**Source:** CHARTER §4

**Classification:** Editorial Principle

**Status:** ADD (new principle)

---

## EP-014: Timeliness Principle (RECOMMENDED)

**Statement:** Publications SHALL be delivered promptly when official data becomes available.

**Source:** CHARTER §2

**Classification:** Editorial Principle

**Status:** ADD (new principle)
