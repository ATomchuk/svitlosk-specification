# EP-001: Reader First Principle

## Canonical Definition

The Telegram Journal exists to serve the residents of the Starokostiantyniv Territorial Community. Every editorial decision SHALL prioritize their informational needs above all other considerations. Publications SHALL be designed for maximum benefit to this specific community, even when the Journal is technically accessible to a wider audience.

## Purpose

This principle governs editorial decisions about audience priority. It determines who benefits from every publication and ensures that community needs drive all editorial choices.

## Does NOT Govern

This principle SHALL NOT regulate:
- How publications are technically delivered
- What data sources are used
- How the Publication Engine operates
- What rendering format is used
- How the Telegram channel is configured

## Relationship Analysis

| Principle | Relationship | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| EP-002 | Independent | Truth applies regardless of audience |
| EP-003 | Independent | Silence applies regardless of audience |
| EP-004 | Independent | Automation applies regardless of audience |
| EP-005 | Independent | Preservation applies regardless of audience |
| EP-007 | Independent | Determinism applies regardless of audience |
| EP-009 | Supports | Territorial Organization serves Reader First |
| EP-010 | Independent | Consistency applies regardless of audience |
| EP-013 | Depends on | Accessibility extends Reader First to all residents |
| EP-014 | Independent | Timeliness applies regardless of audience |

## Semantic Boundary

**Belongs to:** Editorial System only.

**Does NOT belong to:** Publishing Model, Lifecycle Model, Graphic Publication Model.

## Mission Alignment

Directly supports SvitloSk mission: "delivers it promptly to the residents of the Starokostiantyniv Community."

## Professional Benchmark

Aligns with BBC Editorial Guidelines (audience-first principle), Reuters Trust Principles (serving the public interest), and AP Stylebook (audience awareness). Professional standard for public information services.

# EP-002: Editorial Truth Principle

## Canonical Definition

Every publication SHALL faithfully represent the official source without alteration. The Journal SHALL increase clarity by reorganizing information for easier understanding, but SHALL NOT change the factual meaning of officially published data. No assumptions, forecasts, interpretations, or predictions SHALL be presented as facts.

## Purpose

This principle governs editorial decisions about factual integrity. It determines how the Journal relates to its official data sources and ensures that publications remain truthful representations.

## Does NOT Govern

This principle SHALL NOT regulate:
- How data is retrieved from official sources
- How publications are technically rendered
- What formatting is applied
- How the Telegram channel delivers publications
- What lifecycle states publications pass through

## Does NOT Govern

This principle SHALL NOT regulate:
