# TJS-002 — Publication Lifecycle

Status: Draft (Чернетка)

Specification ID: TJS-002

Author: SvitloSk Project

---

# 1. Purpose

This specification defines the complete lifecycle of publications within the official SvitloSk Telegram Journal.

It establishes how information is created, published, updated, archived and, where applicable, removed.

This document is normative.

---

# 2. Scope

This specification applies to every publication automatically generated by the SvitloSk system for the official Telegram Journal.

---

# 3. Lifecycle Overview

Each publication shall pass through one or more of the following states:

1. Generated
2. Scheduled
3. Published
4. Updated
5. Archived
6. Removed (temporary publications only)

---

# 4. Publication Types

The Telegram Journal supports the following publication categories:

- Daily schedule
- Schedule update
- Emergency outage notification
- Emergency restoration notification
- Informational notice
- Technical notice
- System status message

Future publication types may be introduced through new specifications.

---

# 5. Daily Schedule Lifecycle

The daily schedule is generated automatically after official data processing.

Lifecycle:

Generated

↓

Validated

↓

Published

↓

Updated if official data changes

↓

Archived

The daily schedule shall never be deleted.

---

# 6. Emergency Publications

Emergency publications are generated whenever official data indicates an unexpected outage or restoration.

Lifecycle:

Generated

↓

Published immediately

↓

Updated if required

↓

Archived

Emergency publications shall remain available as part of the public historical record.

---

# 7. Temporary Publications

Some informational publications exist only to notify subscribers in advance.

Examples include:

- Tomorrow schedule announcement
- Upcoming publication notice
- Technical maintenance reminder

These publications may be removed automatically after they become irrelevant.

---

# 8. Update Rules

Published messages may be edited only when:

- official source data changes;
- formatting corrections are required;
- generated graphics are regenerated.

Every update shall preserve the original meaning of the official information.

---

# 9. Archive Policy

Historical publications form the permanent public archive of the Telegram Journal.

Archive entries shall not be modified except to correct verified technical errors.

---

# 10. Deletion Policy

Deletion is prohibited for:

- daily schedules;
- emergency notifications;
- restoration notifications;
- official system notices.

Deletion is permitted only for temporary informational publications explicitly designated as removable.

---

# 11. Traceability

Every publication shall be traceable to:

- source dataset;
- publication timestamp;
- generator version;
- generated graphic (if applicable).

---

# 12. Reliability

The Publication Engine shall guarantee that:

- publications are not duplicated;
- publication order is preserved;
- updates target the correct Telegram message;
- archived publications remain accessible.
