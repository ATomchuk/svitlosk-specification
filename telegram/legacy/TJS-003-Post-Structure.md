# TJS-003 — Post Structure

Status: Draft (Чернетка)

Specification ID: TJS-003

Author: SvitloSk Project

---

# 1. Purpose

This specification defines the official structure of publications in the SvitloSk Telegram Journal.

Every automatically generated publication shall conform to the structure defined in this document.

This document is normative.

---

# 2. General Principles

Each publication shall be:

- readable within a few seconds;
- visually consistent;
- concise;
- self-contained;
- understandable without additional explanation.

---

# 3. Standard Publication Structure

The standard publication consists of the following sections:

1. Header
2. Main information
3. Graphic (optional)
4. Additional information
5. Footer

---

# 4. Header

The header identifies the publication.

It shall contain:

- publication type;
- date;
- if applicable, affected schedule.

Example:

Daily Schedule

Emergency Outage

Information

---

# 5. Main Information

The main section contains the essential information.

It shall include only verified information derived from official data.

The main information shall never contain assumptions or predictions.

---

# 6. Graphic Block

If applicable, a publication shall include a generated graphic.

The graphic shall:

- match the published information;
- follow the official SvitloSk visual identity;
- be generated automatically.

---

# 7. Additional Information

Additional information may include:

- source reference;
- explanation;
- notes;
- update information.

This section is optional.

---

# 8. Footer

The footer identifies the publication as part of the official SvitloSk Journal.

The exact footer design is defined by the Visual Identity specification.

---

# 9. Formatting Rules

Publications shall use:

- short paragraphs;
- consistent spacing;
- limited emoji usage;
- uniform typography;
- consistent ordering of sections.

---

# 10. Editing Rules

After publication:

- factual corrections are permitted;
- layout corrections are permitted;
- the meaning of official information shall never change.

---
