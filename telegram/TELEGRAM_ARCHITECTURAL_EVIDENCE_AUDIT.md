## Audit Method

Every conclusion is supported by documentary evidence — exact quotes from the source documents.

Three distinct concepts are evaluated:
- **Duplicated Wording** — identical or near-identical text
- **Duplicated Responsibility** — same semantic responsibility in multiple documents
- **Duplicated Authority** — same concept defined as authoritative in multiple documents

## EB-001: Lifecycle State Model — TJS-002 vs TJS-007 vs TJS-008

**Finding ID:** F-001 (CRITICAL)
**Documents involved:** TJS-002, TJS-007, TJS-008
**Sections involved:** TJS-002 §3, TJS-007 §3, TJS-008 §3

---

**Quoted responsibility from TJS-002 §3:**

> "Each publication shall pass through one or more of the following states:
> 1. Generated
> 2. Scheduled
> 3. Published
> 4. Updated
> 5. Archived
> 6. Removed (temporary publications only)"

**Quoted responsibility from TJS-007 §3:**

> "Every Publication SHALL follow the same lifecycle.
> Dataset → Render → Publish → Update (if required) → Retain or Remove"

**Quoted responsibility from TJS-008 §3:**

> "The Telegram Journal SHALL follow one daily publication cycle.
> The cycle consists of:
> 1. generation of the Today's Package;
> 2. publication of Today's Posts;
> 3. monitoring Dataset updates;
> 4. updating changed Publications;
> 5. generation of Tomorrow Publications;
> 6. removal of obsolete Tomorrow Publications."

---

**Primary Responsibility A (TJS-002):** "Defines the complete lifecycle of publications within the official SvitloSk Telegram Journal." (TJS-002 §1 Purpose)

**Primary Responsibility B (TJS-007):** "Defines the complete lifecycle of Publications within the SvitloSk Telegram Journal." (TJS-007 §1 Purpose)

**Primary Responsibility C (TJS-008):** "Defines the lifecycle of Telegram Journal Publications generated by the SvitloSk Publication Engine." (TJS-008 §1 Purpose)

---

**Architectural Boundary A (TJS-002):** BELONGS: lifecycle states, publication types, daily schedule lifecycle, emergency lifecycle, temporary rules, update rules, archive policy, deletion policy, traceability, reliability. DOES NOT BELONG: daily cycle scheduling, publication windows, ownership model, non-destructive channel principle.

**Architectural Boundary B (TJS-007):** BELONGS: lifecycle states, creation rules, canonical equality, ordering, temporary vs permanent, ownership, non-destructive channel principle, error handling. DOES NOT BELONG: publication types, archive policy, deletion policy, daily cycle scheduling, publication windows, event-driven principle.

**Architectural Boundary C (TJS-008):** BELONGS: daily cycle, packages, publication windows, event-driven principle, stable journal principle, deterministic principle, non-destructive update principle, manual/image exclusion, publication session. DOES NOT BELONG: publication types, archive policy, deletion policy, ownership model, non-destructive channel principle.

---

**Do the boundaries overlap?** **YES** — All three documents claim authority over "lifecycle states" and "lifecycle" as a concept.

**Evidence:**
- TJS-002 Purpose: "defines the complete lifecycle" (line 13)
- TJS-007 Purpose: "defines the complete lifecycle" (line 13)
- TJS-008 Purpose: "defines the lifecycle" (line 13)
- All three use "Publication Lifecycle" as their title
- TJS-002 §3 defines 6 states with state NAMES (Generated, Scheduled, Published, Updated, Archived, Removed)
- TJS-007 §3 defines 5 states with flow NAMES (Dataset → Render → Publish → Update → Retain/Remove)
- TJS-008 §3 defines 6 STEPS with action NAMES (generation, publication, monitoring, updating, generation, removal)

**Reasoning:** The three documents define three DIFFERENT lifecycle models. TJS-002 uses state-based terminology. TJS-007 uses flow-based terminology. TJS-008 uses cycle-based terminology. The state counts differ (6, 5, 6). The state names differ. Yet all three claim to define "the lifecycle." This is not duplicated wording — the text is different. This is duplicated AUTHORITY — all three claim to be the authoritative lifecycle definition.

**Conclusion:** **CONFIRMED** — REAL SSOT VIOLATION. Three documents define three different lifecycle state models, each claiming authority. This creates architectural ambiguity about which model is canonical.

**Classification:** **(A) REAL SSOT VIOLATION**

**Architectural Confidence:** **HIGH** — Documentary evidence is unambiguous. All three Purpose statements claim "defines the complete lifecycle." All three define lifecycle state models with different state counts and names.

## EB-002: Update Rules — TJS-002 vs TJS-007 vs TJS-008

**Finding ID:** F-002 (CRITICAL)
**Documents involved:** TJS-002, TJS-007, TJS-008
**Sections involved:** TJS-002 §8, TJS-007 §5, TJS-008 §11

---

**Quoted responsibility from TJS-002 §8:**

> "Published messages may be edited only when:
> - official source data changes;
> - formatting corrections are required;
> - generated graphics are regenerated.
> Every update shall preserve the original meaning of the official information."

**Quoted responsibility from TJS-007 §5:**

> "A Publication MAY be updated only when:
> - the normalized dataset changes; or
> - the rendering rules change.
> Updates SHALL be performed by editing the existing Telegram Message.
> A new Publication SHALL NOT be created if an existing Publication can be updated."

**Quoted responsibility from TJS-008 §11:**

> "A Publication SHALL be updated only when:
> - the normalized Dataset changes; or
> - the Rendering Rules change.
> No other event SHALL trigger Publication updates."

---

**Primary Responsibility A (TJS-002 §8):** Defines when published messages may be edited.

**Primary Responsibility B (TJS-007 §5):** Defines when Publications may be updated.

**Primary Responsibility C (TJS-008 §11):** Defines when Publications shall be updated.

---

**Do the boundaries overlap?** **YES** — All three define the same responsibility: "when updates are permitted."

**Evidence:**
- TJS-002 §8: 3 conditions (source data, formatting, graphics)
- TJS-007 §5: 2 conditions (dataset, rendering rules)
- TJS-008 §11: 2 conditions (dataset, Rendering Rules)
- TJS-002 includes "formatting corrections" and "generated graphics" — TJS-007 and TJS-008 do NOT
- TJS-007 and TJS-008 add "No other event SHALL trigger" (TJS-008) and "A new Publication SHALL NOT be created if an existing Publication can be updated" (TJS-007) — TJS-002 does NOT

**Reasoning:** The update conditions are DIFFERENT across the three documents. TJS-002 is broader (3 conditions). TJS-007 and TJS-008 are narrower (2 conditions) but add additional constraints. This creates ambiguity: can formatting corrections trigger updates? Can graphic regeneration trigger updates? The answer depends on which document you consult.

**Conclusion:** **CONFIRMED** — REAL SSOT VIOLATION. Three documents define different sets of update conditions, each claiming authority.

**Classification:** **(A) REAL SSOT VIOLATION**

**Architectural Confidence:** **HIGH** — Documentary evidence is unambiguous. The three sets of conditions are explicitly different.

## EB-004: Archive/Permanent Rules — TJS-002 vs TJS-007

**Finding ID:** F-004 (MAJOR)
**Documents involved:** TJS-002, TJS-007
**Sections involved:** TJS-002 §9, TJS-007 §9

---

**Quoted responsibility from TJS-002 §9:**

> "Historical publications form the permanent public archive of the Telegram Journal.
> Archive entries shall not be modified except to correct verified technical errors."

**Quoted responsibility from TJS-007 §9:**

> "Current-day Publications SHALL remain in the Telegram Journal.
> The Publisher SHALL NOT remove historical Publications generated for the current reporting day.
> The Telegram Journal serves as the historical record of daily Publications."

---

**Do the boundaries overlap?** **PARTIALLY** — Both define preservation rules but at different lifecycle stages.

**Evidence:**
- TJS-002 §9: Defines ARCHIVE policy — "Historical publications form the permanent public archive"
- TJS-007 §9: Defines PERMANENT classification — "Current-day Publications SHALL remain"
- TJS-002 addresses HISTORICAL records (after archival)
- TJS-007 addresses CURRENT-DAY records (before archival)

**Reasoning:** These are related but not identical responsibilities. TJS-002 defines what happens AFTER archival (don't modify). TJS-007 defines what happens DURING the reporting day (don't remove). However, both claim authority over "preservation" of publications. The TJS-007 §9 statement "The Telegram Journal serves as the historical record of daily Publications" overlaps with TJS-002 §9 "Historical publications form the permanent public archive."

**Conclusion:** **PARTIALLY CONFIRMED** — The overlap is real but the responsibilities are at different lifecycle stages. Both documents claim authority over preservation, but with different temporal scopes.

**Classification:** **(D) REQUIRED EVIDENCE** — Same fact (preservation) used in different lifecycle contexts.

**Architectural Confidence:** **MEDIUM** — The responsibilities are related but distinguishable by temporal scope.

## EB-010: Non-Destructive Principles — TJS-007 vs TJS-008

**Finding ID:** F-010 (MINOR)
**Documents involved:** TJS-007, TJS-008
**Sections involved:** TJS-007 §11, TJS-008 §10

---

**Quoted responsibility from TJS-007 §11:**

> "Non-destructive Channel Principle
> The Telegram Journal SHALL be treated as a long-lived public information journal.
> The Publisher SHALL modify only Publications that it owns.
> The Publisher SHALL NOT modify, delete or reorder Publications created by other publishers or administrators."

**Quoted responsibility from TJS-008 §10:**

> "Non-destructive Update Principle
> Updates SHOULD modify existing Publications instead of replacing them.
> Replacing an existing Publication SHALL be considered a last resort."

---

**Do the boundaries overlap?** **NO** — These are DIFFERENT principles.

**Evidence:**
- TJS-007 §11: "Non-destructive CHANNEL Principle" — about WHAT you may touch (only your own posts)
- TJS-008 §10: "Non-destructive UPDATE Principle" — about HOW you may change (update in place, don't recreate)
- TJS-007 addresses OWNERSHIP (which posts the Publisher may modify)
- TJS-008 addresses STRATEGY (edit vs delete+recreate)
- The names are different: "Channel" vs "Update"
- The content is different: ownership rules vs update strategy

**Reasoning:** These are RELATED but DISTINCT principles. TJS-007's principle is about channel ownership — the Publisher must not touch posts it doesn't own. TJS-008's principle is about update strategy — prefer editing over recreating. They address different architectural concerns. However, both belong in a lifecycle document and would benefit from being co-located.

**Conclusion:** **NOT a SSOT VIOLATION** — Different principles with different concerns.

**Classification:** **(B) CONTEXTUAL REUSE** — Related principles in different documents.

**Architectural Confidence:** **HIGH** — The principles have different names, different content, and address different concerns.
