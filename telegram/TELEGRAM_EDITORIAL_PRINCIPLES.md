# Purpose

This document harvests every editorial principle governing the Editorial System of the Telegram Publishing System.

---

# EP-001: Reader First Principle

**Statement:** The primary audience is the residents of the Starokostiantyniv Territorial Community. All editorial decisions shall prioritize the informational needs of this community.

**Source:** TJS-001 §8

**Type:** Audience principle

---

# EP-002: Editorial Truth Principle

**Statement:** Every publication must increase clarity without changing the meaning of the official source. The journal does not interpret, predict or alter official information.

**Source:** TJS-001 §6

**Type:** Integrity principle

---

# EP-003: Editorial Silence Principle

**Statement:** SvitloSk does not modify open data. SvitloSk does not create new facts. SvitloSk does not produce forecasts. SvitloSk does not alter the meaning of officially published information.

**Source:** CHARTER §7

**Type:** Integrity principle

---

# EP-004: Minimal Editorial Intervention Principle

**Statement:** Every publication is generated automatically from structured data. Manual editing is not part of the standard publication workflow. After publication: factual corrections are permitted; layout corrections are permitted; the meaning of official information shall never change.

**Source:** TJS-004 §Automatic, TJS-003 §10

**Type:** Workflow principle

---

# EP-005: Issue Integrity Principle

**Statement:** The Telegram Journal serves as the permanent public archive of publications related to the current day. Daily publications concerning planned and emergency outages remain available as part of the historical record.

**Source:** TJS-001 §11

**Type:** Preservation principle

---

# EP-006: Source Fidelity Principle

**Statement:** Every publication must increase clarity without changing the meaning of the official source. The project interprets data without changing their factual meaning. No assumptions, forecasts or predictions SHALL be presented as facts.

**Source:** TJS-001 §6, PP P-03

**Type:** Integrity principle

---

# EP-007: Deterministic Editorial Behaviour Principle

**Statement:** Two identical normalized Datasets SHALL always generate identical Telegram Journals. Publication order, formatting and content SHALL remain deterministic.

**Source:** TJS-008 §9

**Type:** Quality principle

---

# EP-008: Accuracy Before Speed Principle

**Statement:** Publishing information quickly is important. Publishing accurate information is mandatory. Whenever these goals conflict, accuracy SHALL take priority.

**Source:** PP P-04

**Type:** Quality principle

---

# EP-009: Territory-First Principle

**Statement:** Territory name is always the first element of the publication. A post represents only one territory.

**Source:** TJS-004 §Territory

**Type:** Structure principle

---

# EP-010: Consistency Principle

**Statement:** The objective is to ensure that every publication is consistent, easy to read, predictable and automatically generated from structured data.

**Source:** TJS-004 §Purpose

**Type:** Quality principle

---

# EP-011: Transparency Principle

**Statement:** The project SHALL remain transparent. Its documentation, architecture, terminology and engineering principles SHALL be publicly available.

**Source:** PP P-06

**Type:** Governance principle

---

# EP-012: Community-First Principle

**Statement:** Although the journal is publicly accessible, all editorial decisions shall prioritize the informational needs of the Starokostiantyniv Community.

**Source:** TJS-001 §8

**Type:** Audience principle
