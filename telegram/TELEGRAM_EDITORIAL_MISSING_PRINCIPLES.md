# Purpose

This document identifies editorial principles that are NOT currently harvested but may improve the Editorial Model. Only principles clearly beneficial for SvitloSk are recommended. Enterprise complexity is avoided.

---

# Candidate Principles

## MP-001: Accessibility Principle

**Statement:** Publications SHALL be accessible to all residents regardless of technical experience or device.

**Source:** CHARTER §4 (Social Mission: "provides equal access to information")

**Why beneficial:**
- SvitloSk serves a community with varying technical literacy
- Telegram is accessible on smartphones, tablets, and desktops
- Publications must be readable without special software or knowledge
- Supports the social mission of equal access

**Classification:** Editorial Principle

**Mission Compatibility:** 5/5 — Directly supports social mission

**Recommendation:** ADD as EP-013

---

## MP-002: Timeliness Principle

**Statement:** Publications SHALL be delivered promptly when official data becomes available.

**Source:** CHARTER §2 (Mission: "delivers it promptly to the residents")

**Why beneficial:**
- Timeliness is a core part of the mission
- Residents need timely information about outages
- An explicit timeliness principle reinforces this
- Supports the "Accuracy Before Speed" principle by clarifying that timely delivery is still important

**Classification:** Editorial Principle

**Mission Compatibility:** 5/5 — Directly supports mission

**Recommendation:** ADD as EP-014

---

## MP-003: Completeness Principle

**Statement:** Publications SHALL include all relevant information from the official source for the reporting period.

**Source:** Implicit in TJS-004 §Categories (all categories without records are omitted)

**Why beneficial:**
- Ensures no information is silently dropped
- Reinforces "Source Fidelity" by requiring completeness
- Supports transparency and traceability

**Classification:** Editorial Principle

**Mission Compatibility:** 4/5 — Supports transparency and completeness

**Recommendation:** CONSIDER — may be covered by EP-010 (Consistency) and EP-006 (Source Fidelity)

---

## MP-004: Neutrality Principle

**Statement:** Publications SHALL not express opinions, preferences, or judgments about the information presented.

**Source:** Implicit in TJS-001 §6 ("does not interpret, predict or alter") and CHARTER §7 ("does not alter the meaning")

**Why beneficial:**
- Reinforces editorial neutrality
- Ensures objective presentation of official data
- Supports trust and credibility

**Classification:** Editorial Principle

**Mission Compatibility:** 5/5 — Supports neutrality and trust

**Recommendation:** CONSIDER — may be covered by EP-002 (Editorial Truth) and EP-003 (Editorial Silence)
