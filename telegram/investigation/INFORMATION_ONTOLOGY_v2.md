## Models of Information

### Model A: Information as Object

**Definition:** Information is a thing that can be created, stored, transmitted.

**Evidence:**

- Information can be written down
- Information can be transmitted
- Information can be archived

**Fit:** PARTIAL — explains information as artifact but not as concept.

---

### Model B: Information as Relation

**Definition:** Information is a relationship between Reality and a Knower.

**Evidence:**

- Information connects Reality to Knowledge
- Information requires both source and receiver
- Information is about something

**Fit:** GOOD — explains why information requires observation.

---

### Model C: Information as Difference

**Definition:** Information is a difference that makes a difference (Bateson).

**Evidence:**

- Information changes what Knower knows
- Information reduces uncertainty
- Information has value because it changes something

**Fit:** GOOD — explains information value.

---

### Model D: Information as Property

**Definition:** Information is an attribute of data or communication.

**Evidence:**

- Data has information content
- Communication carries information
- Information is a characteristic

**Fit:** PARTIAL — explains information as attribute but not as entity.

---

### Model E: Information as Signal

**Definition:** Information is a signal that conveys meaning.

**Evidence:**

- Signals carry information
- Information is decoded from signals
- Information is the meaning behind the signal

**Fit:** PARTIAL — explains information transmission but not origin.

---

### Model F: Information as Meaning

**Definition:** Information is the meaning conveyed by a message.

**Evidence:**

- Messages carry meaning
- Meaning is what receiver understands
- Information is the semantic content

**Fit:** PARTIAL — explains information semantics but not structure.

# Key Insight

**Information is BEST described as a RELATION between Reality and a Knower (Model B) or as a DIFFERENCE that makes a difference (Model C).**

**Information requires BOTH Reality (subject) and a Knower (observer).**

**Information is NOT a thing that exists independently — it exists in the RELATIONSHIP between Reality and Knowledge.**

**Information comes AFTER Knowledge in the chain.**
