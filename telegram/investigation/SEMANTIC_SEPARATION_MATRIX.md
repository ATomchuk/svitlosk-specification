# Separation Analysis

## Are the Six Concepts Mutually Exclusive?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

| Concept | Definition | Distinct From |
|---------|------------|---------------|
| Reality | Physical existence | Not a claim, belief, relationship, portrayal, or medium |
| Fact | Claim about Reality | Not Reality itself, not belief, not relationship |
| Knowledge | Belief held by Knower | Not Reality, not claim, not relationship |
| Information | Relationship between Reality and Knower | Not Reality, not claim, not belief |
| Representation | Portrayal of Information | Not Information itself, not medium |
| Carrier | Medium holding Representation | Not Representation, not Information |

## Can Any Two Concepts Be Confused?

| Pair | Confusable? | Evidence |

| Reality vs Fact | NO | Reality is physical; Fact is claim |
| Reality vs Knowledge | NO | Reality is physical; Knowledge is belief |
| Reality vs Information | NO | Reality is physical; Information is relationship |
| Fact vs Knowledge | PARTIALLY | Fact is claim; Knowledge is belief — similar but different |
| Fact vs Information | PARTIALLY | Fact is claim; Information is relationship — similar but different |
| Knowledge vs Information | PARTIALLY | Knowledge is belief; Information is relationship — similar but different |
| Information vs Representation | PARTIALLY | Information is content; Representation is form — related but different |
| Representation vs Carrier | PARTIALLY | Representation is form; Carrier is medium — related but different |

# Key Insight

**All six concepts are MUTUALLY EXCLUSIVE.**

**Each concept has distinct ontological properties.**

**The concepts form a CHAIN: Reality → Fact → Knowledge → Information → Representation → Carrier.**

**Each concept depends on the previous one but is not the same as it.**
