# Experiment 1: Remove Issue

## Setup

Remove Issue from the ontology completely.

## Questions

### Can Publication still exist?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Publication is defined as "A public information message generated by the SvitloSk system" (GLOSSARY.md §3)
- Publication has its own identity (Territory + Date + Template)
- Publication has its own lifecycle (Generated → Published → Updated → Archived → Removed)
- Publication is owned by Publication Engine

**Analysis:** Publication can exist without Issue. Issue provides grouping, not existence.

---

### Can Journal still exist?

**Answer:** YES, but differently

**Evidence:**

- Journal is defined as "The continuous informational publication of the SvitloSk project" (TJS-000 §4)
- Journal contains Issues (TJS-000 §4 hierarchy)

**Analysis:** Journal can exist without Issues, but would lose its daily structure. Journal would become a flat collection of Publications.

---

### Can Publication Engine still function?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Publication Engine creates Publications (TJS-010 §4.1)
- Publication Engine delivers Publications to Telegram (TJS-010 §3.3)
- Publication Engine does not depend on Issue for core functionality

**Analysis:** Publication Engine can function without Issue. Issue is not required for core publishing.

---

## Consequences of Removing Issue

| Consequence | Severity |
|-------------|----------|
| Loss of daily grouping | HIGH |
| Loss of editorial integrity | HIGH |
| Loss of historical record structure | MEDIUM |
| Loss of Journal hierarchy | MEDIUM |
| Publication Engine unaffected | LOW |

# Experiment 2: Remove Publication

## Setup

Remove Publication from the ontology completely.

## Questions

### Can Issue still exist?

**Answer:** NO

**Evidence:**

- TJS-021 §8.1: "An Issue opens when the first Publication for a calendar day is generated"
- Issue requires at least one Publication to exist
- Issue is triggered by Publication

**Analysis:** Issue cannot exist without Publication. Issue is dependent on Publication.

---

### Can Journal still exist?

**Answer:** YES, but empty

**Evidence:**

- Journal is defined as continuous publication
- Journal can exist without Issues (but would be empty)

**Analysis:** Journal can exist without Issues, but would have no content.

---

### Can Publication Engine still function?

**Answer:** YES, but produces nothing

**Evidence:**

- Publication Engine creates Publications
- Without Publications, Engine has nothing to create

**Analysis:** Publication Engine can exist but produces no output.

---

## Consequences of Removing Publication

| Consequence | Severity |
|-------------|----------|
| Issue cannot exist | CRITICAL |
| Journal becomes empty | HIGH |
| Publication Engine produces nothing | HIGH |
| Telegram has no content | HIGH |

---

# Comparison

| Experiment | Issue | Publication | Journal | Engine |
|------------|-------|-------------|---------|--------|
| Remove Issue | N/A | CAN exist | Exists (flat) | Functions |
| Remove Publication | CANNOT exist | N/A | Exists (empty) | Produces nothing |

---

# Key Insight

**Issue depends on Publication more than Publication depends on Issue.**

**Issue is DERIVATIVE — it exists because Publications exist.**

**Publication is FOUNDATIONAL — it can exist without Issue.**
