## Models of Fact

### Model 1: Fact as Reality State

**Definition:** Fact is a state of Reality that exists independently.

**Evidence:**

- "The power is out" describes Reality
- Reality exists independently of the claim

**Fit:** PARTIAL — explains true Facts but not false Facts.

---

### Model 2: Fact as Claim

**Definition:** Fact is a claim made about Reality.

**Evidence:**

- "The power is out" is a CLAIM
- Claim can be true or false
- Claim requires a claimant

**Fit:** GOOD — explains both true and false Facts.

---

### Model 3: Fact as Interpretation

**Definition:** Fact is an interpretation of Reality.

**Evidence:**

- Different people interpret Reality differently
- Different Facts can describe same Reality
- Facts are not Reality itself

**Fit:** GOOD — explains why Facts can differ.

---

### Model 4: Fact as Evidence

**Definition:** Fact is evidence of Reality.

**Evidence:**

- Facts provide evidence of what happened
- Facts can be used to verify claims
- Facts are traceable to sources

**Fit:** PARTIAL — explains factual evidence but not interpretation.

# Key Insight

**Fact is an INTERPRETATION of Reality made by a claimant.**

**Fact is NOT Reality itself — it is a CLAIM about Reality.**

**Fact can be true or false, can disappear, can be contradicted.**

**Fact requires a claimant to exist.**
