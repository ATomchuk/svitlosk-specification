# Purpose

This document constructs at least THREE different semantic interpretations of Publication without recommending one.

---

# Model M-001: Publication as Message

## What Publication Is

Publication is a MESSAGE — a discrete unit of information sent from sender to receiver through a communication channel.

## Why It Fits

**Evidence:**

- GLOSSARY.md: "A public information message generated by the SvitloSk system"
- TJS-000A: "a discrete unit of information delivered to readers through the publication channel"
- The word "message" appears in the canonical definition
- Publications are delivered through Telegram (a messaging platform)
- Publications have a sender (SvitloSk) and receivers (Subscribers)
- Publications are one-way communication (no response expected)

**Supporting Sources:** GLOSSARY.md, TJS-000A, TJS-010

## Why It Does Not Fit

**Contradicting Evidence:**

- Publications have complex lifecycle (Generated → Published → Updated → Archived) — messages typically don't
- Publications are deterministic — messages are typically not
- Publications have territorial grammar — messages typically don't
- Publications are archived permanently — messages typically aren't
- Publications can be updated in place — messages typically aren't
- The Telegram Glossary explicitly forbids using "Message" as a synonym for Publication (§17)

**Contradicting Sources:** TJS-021, TJS-000A §17, TJS-010

# Model M-002: Publication as Document

## What Publication Is

Publication is a DOCUMENT — a structured, persistent, authoritative record of information.

## Why It Fits

**Evidence:**

- DATA_MODEL.md: "Logical representation of interpreted information prepared for public distribution"
- Publications have structure (Territory Header → Sections → Settlement → Time → Street → Addresses)
- Publications are persistent (archived permanently)
- Publications are authoritative (traceable to official sources)
- Publications have versions (can be updated)
- Publications follow editorial standards
- Publications are part of a Journal (like articles in a journal)

**Supporting Sources:** DATA_MODEL.md, TJS-005, TJS-020

## Why It Does Not Fit

**Contradicting Evidence:**

- Documents are typically static — Publications are dynamic (can be updated)
- Documents have authors — Publications are auto-generated
- Documents have unique content — Publications can be deterministic (same input = same output)
- Documents are typically read once — Publications are monitored and updated
- Documents don't have Telegram Message IDs — Publications do
- Documents don't have lifecycle states like "Removed" — Publications do

**Contradicting Sources:** TJS-021, TJS-010, Legacy TJS-007

# Model M-003: Publication as Event

## What Publication Is

Publication is an EVENT — a discrete occurrence in time that represents a state change in the system.

## Why It Fits

**Evidence:**

- Publications are triggered by events (Dataset changes)
- Publications have timestamps (generation time, publication time, update time)
- Publications mark transitions (from "no information" to "information available")
- Publications are part of a sequence (daily publication cycle)
- Publications have temporal relevance (today vs tomorrow)
- Publications expire (temporary ones are removed)

**Supporting Sources:** TJS-010, TJS-021, Legacy TJS-008

## Why It Does Not Fit

**Contradicting Evidence:**

- Events are typically instantaneous — Publications persist over time
- Events are not updated — Publications are updated in place
- Events don't have content — Publications have structured content
- Events don't have territory — Publications represent exactly one Territory
- Events don't have templates — Publications use Canonical Templates
- Events don't have rendering rules — Publications are rendered deterministically

**Contradicting Sources:** TJS-005, TJS-006, TJS-022

---

# Model M-004: Publication as Information Object

## What Publication Is

Publication is an INFORMATION OBJECT — a self-contained unit of structured information with defined behavior and lifecycle.

## Why It Fits

**Evidence:**

- Publications contain structured information (Territory, Settlements, Streets, Addresses, Time Intervals)
- Publications have defined behavior (creation, update, archival, removal)
- Publications have identity (Telegram Message ID, Publication Identity)
- Publications have relationships (belong to Issue, contain in Package)
- Publications have invariants (deterministic, traceable, non-destructive)
- Publications are the semantic center of the ontology (governance analysis)

**Supporting Sources:** TJS-000A, TJS-010, TJS-021, governance/SEMANTIC_CENTER_ANALYSIS.md

## Why It Does Not Fit

**Contradicting Evidence:**

- Information Objects are typically passive — Publications are actively managed
- Information Objects don't have platform dependencies — Publications have Telegram Message IDs
- Information Objects don't have delivery mechanisms — Publications are delivered through Telegram
- Information Objects are typically immutable — Publications are updated in place
- Information Objects don't have editorial policies — Publications follow editorial standards

**Contradicting Sources:** TJS-010, TJS-020, Legacy TJS-007

---

# Model M-005: Publication as Business Object

## What Publication Is

Publication is a BUSINESS OBJECT — a domain entity that represents a key business concept with defined rules and constraints.

## Why It Fits

**Evidence:**

- Publications are central to the business (SvitloSk exists to create Publications)
- Publications have business rules (Editorial Policy, Formatting Rules)
- Publications have stakeholders (Residents, Administrators, SvitloSk team)
- Publications have value (informing residents about outages)
- Publications have quality requirements (deterministic, traceable, accessible)
- Publications are governed by business principles (Reader First, Editorial Truth, etc.)

**Supporting Sources:** CHARTER.md, TJS-020, PROJECT_PRINCIPLES.md

## Why It Does Not Fit

**Contradicting Evidence:**

- Business Objects are typically created by business actors — Publications are auto-generated
- Business Objects typically have mutable state managed by users — Publications are system-managed
- Business Objects typically don't have rendering rules — Publications do
- Business Objects typically don't have platform dependencies — Publications have Telegram Message IDs
- Business Objects are typically part of business processes — Publications ARE the business process output

**Contradicting Sources:** TJS-010, TJS-021, Legacy TJS-008

---

# Model M-006: Publication as Editorial Artifact

## What Publication Is

Publication is an EDITORIAL ARTIFACT — a product of editorial process that follows defined standards and conventions.

## Why It Fits

**Evidence:**

- Publications follow Editorial Policy (TJS-004, TJS-020)
- Publications have formatting rules (TJS-005, TJS-006)
- Publications have editorial principles (Reader First, Editorial Truth, etc.)
- Publications are part of a Journal (editorial publication)
- Publications have territorial organization (editorial structure)
- Publications are generated automatically (editorial automation)

**Supporting Sources:** TJS-020, Legacy TJS-004, TJS-005

## Why It Does Not Fit

**Contradicting Evidence:**

- Editorial Artifacts are typically created by editors — Publications are auto-generated
- Editorial Artifacts typically have human judgment — Publications are deterministic
- Editorial Artifacts typically have creative content — Publications are structured data
- Editorial Artifacts typically don't have lifecycle states — Publications do
- Editorial Artifacts typically don't have platform dependencies — Publications have Telegram Message IDs

**Contradicting Sources:** TJS-010, TJS-021, Legacy TJS-007

---

# Model M-007: Publication as Communication Unit

## What Publication Is

Publication is a COMMUNICATION UNIT — a standardized unit of communication designed for mass distribution.

## Why It Fits

**Evidence:**

- Publications are distributed to many Subscribers simultaneously
- Publications follow consistent format (deterministic rendering)
- Publications are designed for readability (Editorial Principles)
- Publications are accessible to all residents (Accessibility principle)
- Publications are timely (Timeliness principle)
- Publications are part of a communication channel (Telegram)

**Supporting Sources:** TJS-020, GLOSSARY.md, CHARTER.md

## Why It Does Not Fit

**Contradicting Evidence:**

- Communication Units are typically one-way — Publications can be updated based on feedback (data changes)
- Communication Units typically don't have complex lifecycle — Publications do
- Communication Units typically don't have territorial grammar — Publications do
- Communication Units typically don't have deterministic rendering — Publications do
- Communication Units typically don't have archive requirements — Publications do

**Contradicting Sources:** TJS-021, TJS-005, TJS-022
