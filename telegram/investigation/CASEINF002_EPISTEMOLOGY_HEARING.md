# Purpose

# Part I: Reality

## What Exists Independently?

**Answer:** Physical phenomena, locations, people, organizations.

**Evidence:**

- Power outage occurs whether or not anyone observes it
- Addresses exist as physical locations regardless of observation
- DSO exists as an organization regardless of SvitloSk

**Conclusion:** Reality EXISTS independently of humans and systems.

---

## What Exists Before Observation?

**Answer:** Reality exists before observation.

**Evidence:**

- Power outage occurs before dispatcher observes
- Electricity availability is physical state regardless of observation
- Addresses exist as physical locations before anyone notes them

**Conclusion:** Reality EXISTED before any observation.

---

## Can Reality Change Because of Information?

**Answer:** NO

**Evidence:**

- Reality changes based on physical causes (weather, equipment failure)
- Information does not cause Reality to change
- Information DESCRIBES Reality, does not CREATE it

**Conclusion:** Reality is INDEPENDENT of Information.

---

## Can Reality Exist Without Observers?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Power outage occurs whether or not anyone knows about it
- Electricity availability is physical state regardless of observers
- Reality exists in the universe independent of consciousness

**Conclusion:** Reality EXISTS without observers.

---

# Part II: Fact

## Is Fact Part of Reality?

**Answer:** PARTIALLY

**Evidence:**

- Fact describes a state of Reality
- Fact is ABOUT Reality, not Reality itself
- Fact requires interpretation to exist

**Conclusion:** Fact is a DESCRIPTION of Reality, not Reality itself.

---

## Is Fact Already Interpretation?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Fact requires someone to state it
- Fact is a CLAIM about Reality
- Fact can be true or false
- Different people may state different Facts about same Reality

**Conclusion:** Fact IS an INTERPRETATION of Reality.

---

## Can Facts Exist Without People?

**Answer:** NO

**Evidence:**

- Fact requires a CLAIMANT
- Fact is a statement made by someone
- Without people, there are no Facts, only Reality

**Conclusion:** Facts REQUIRE people to exist.

---

## Can a Fact Disappear?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Facts can be forgotten
- Facts can be retracted
- Facts can be proven wrong
- But Reality that Fact described may still exist

**Conclusion:** Facts CAN disappear; Reality may persist.

---

## Can the Same Reality Generate Multiple Facts?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Different people observe same Reality differently
- Different people state different Facts about same Reality
- Facts can contradict each other

**Conclusion:** Same Reality CAN generate multiple, even contradictory, Facts.

---

# Part III: Knowledge

## What Exactly Is Knowledge?

**Definition:** Knowledge is justified true belief about Reality.

**Evidence:**

- Knowledge requires a KNOWER
- Knowledge requires a SUBJECT (Reality)
- Knowledge can be true or false
- Knowledge is held by a person or system

**Conclusion:** Knowledge is BELIEF held by a Knower about Reality.

---

## Who Owns Knowledge?

**Answer:** The Knower.

**Evidence:**

- Knowledge exists in minds (or systems)
- Knowledge is held by individuals
- Knowledge can be transferred between Knowers

**Conclusion:** Knowledge is OWNED by the Knower.

---

## Can Two Actors Possess Different Knowledge About the Same Reality?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- DSO knows about outage
- Resident does not know about outage
- Different Knowers have different Knowledge

**Conclusion:** Different Knowers CAN have different Knowledge.

---

## Can Knowledge Be Wrong?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Knowledge is belief, not truth
- Beliefs can be incorrect
- Knowledge can be based on false Facts

**Conclusion:** Knowledge CAN be wrong.

---

## Can Knowledge Disappear?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Knowledge can be forgotten
- Knowledge can be lost
- Knowledge depends on the Knower's mind

**Conclusion:** Knowledge CAN disappear.

---

# Part IV: Information

## Reconstructed from First Principles

### Model A: Information is an Object

**Definition:** Information is a thing that can be created, stored, transmitted.

**Evidence:**

- Information can be written down
- Information can be transmitted
- Information can be archived

**Fit:** PARTIAL — explains information as artifact but not as concept.

---

### Model B: Information is a Relation

**Definition:** Information is a relationship between Reality and a Knower.

**Evidence:**

- Information connects Reality to Knowledge
- Information requires both source and receiver
- Information is about something

**Fit:** GOOD — explains why information requires observation.

---

### Model C: Information is a Difference

**Definition:** Information is a difference that makes a difference (Bateson).

**Evidence:**

- Information changes what Knower knows
- Information reduces uncertainty
- Information has value because it changes something

**Fit:** GOOD — explains information value.

---

### Model D: Information is a Property

**Definition:** Information is an attribute of data or communication.

**Evidence:**

- Data has information content
- Communication carries information
- Information is a characteristic

**Fit:** PARTIAL — explains information as attribute but not as entity.

---

### Model E: Information is a Signal

**Definition:** Information is a signal that conveys meaning.

**Evidence:**

- Signals carry information
- Information is decoded from signals
- Information is the meaning behind the signal

**Fit:** PARTIAL — explains information transmission but not origin.

---

### Model F: Information is a Meaning

**Definition:** Information is the meaning conveyed by a message.

**Evidence:**

- Messages carry meaning
- Meaning is what receiver understands
- Information is the semantic content

**Fit:** PARTIAL — explains information semantics but not structure.

---

# Part V: Representation

## What Is Representation?

**Definition:** Representation is a portrayal of Information in a specific format or medium.

**Evidence:**

- Information can be represented as text, image, audio
- Representation is the FORM, not the CONTENT
- Multiple Representations can convey same Information

**Conclusion:** Representation is the FORM of Information.

---

## Does Representation Contain Information?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Telegram Message contains Publication content
- Database row contains data
- Markdown contains formatted text

**Conclusion:** Representation CONTAINS Information.

---

## Does Representation Merely Reference Information?

**Answer:** NO

**Evidence:**

- Representation contains the actual content
- Representation is not just a pointer
- Representation carries the Information itself

**Conclusion:** Representation CONTAINS, not merely references.

---

## Can Multiple Representations Describe One Information?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Same outage can be text, image, or schedule
- Same data can be JSON, XML, or CSV
- Same Publication can be Telegram Message or Repository Object

**Conclusion:** Multiple Representations CAN describe same Information.

---

# Part VI: Carrier

## What Is Carrier?

**Definition:** Carrier is the physical or digital medium that holds Representation.

**Examples:**

- Telegram Message (digital carrier)
- JSON (data carrier)
- Database row (storage carrier)
- Markdown (text carrier)
- Image (visual carrier)

---

## Is Carrier Information?

**Answer:** NO

**Evidence:**

- Carrier is the MEDIUM, not the MESSAGE
- Carrier can be empty
- Carrier exists without Information

**Conclusion:** Carrier is NOT Information. Carrier is the MEDIUM.

---

## Does Carrier Only Store Information?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Carrier holds Representation
- Carrier provides persistence
- Carrier enables transmission

**Conclusion:** Carrier STORES and TRANSMITS Information.

---

## Can Carrier Change While Information Remains Identical?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Same Publication can be Telegram Message or Repository Object
- Same data can be JSON or XML
- Same content can be different formats

**Conclusion:** Carrier CAN change while Information remains identical.

# Part IX: Collapse Experiments

## Experiment 1: Remove Knowledge

**Question:** Can Information exist?

**Answer:** NO

**Evidence:**

- Information requires a Knower
- Knowledge is what Knower holds
- Without Knowledge, no Information

**Verdict:** Knowledge is ESSENTIAL.

---

## Experiment 2: Remove Information

**Question:** Can Representation exist?

**Answer:** NO

**Evidence:**

- Representation portrays Information
- Without Information, Representation has no content
- Representation cannot exist without Information

**Verdict:** Information is ESSENTIAL.

---

## Experiment 3: Remove Representation

**Question:** Can Carrier exist?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Carrier can be empty
- Carrier exists without content
- Carrier is the medium, not the message

**Verdict:** Representation is NOT essential for Carrier.

---

## Experiment 4: Remove Carrier

**Question:** Can Publication still exist?

**Answer:** PARTIALLY

**Evidence:**

- Publication is Information + Representation
- Publication can exist conceptually without Carrier
- But Publication cannot be distributed without Carrier

**Verdict:** Carrier is essential for DISTRIBUTION but not for CONCEPT.

**Shannon's view:** Information is measure of uncertainty reduction.

**Our ontology:** Information is relationship between Reality and Knower (Model B) or difference that makes difference (Model C).

**Comparison:** PARTIAL overlap — Shannon focuses on quantification; our ontology focuses on semantics.

---

## Gregory Bateson

**Bateson's view:** Information is "a difference that makes a difference."

**Our ontology:** Model C matches Bateson exactly.

**Comparison:** GOOD match — Bateson's definition aligns with our Model C.

---

## Karl Popper

**Popper's view:** World 1 (physical), World 2 (mental), World 3 (objective knowledge).

**Our ontology:** Reality = World 1; Knowledge = World 2; Information ≈ World 3.

**Comparison:** GOOD match — our ontology aligns with Popper's three worlds.

---

## Michael Buckland

**Buckland's view:** Information as (1) knowledge, (2) thing, (3) process.

**Our ontology:** Knowledge = knowledge; Information = thing; Observation = process.

**Comparison:** GOOD match — our ontology covers all three Buckland aspects.

---

**Our ontology:** Reality → Fact → Knowledge → Information → Representation → Carrier

---

**Our ontology:** Information is relationship between Reality and Knower.

**Evidence:**

- Reality: physical existence
- Fact: claim about Reality
- Knowledge: belief held by Knower
- Information: relationship between Reality and Knower
- Representation: portrayal of Information
- Carrier: medium holding Representation

**Each concept has distinct ontological properties.**

---

**Evidence:**

- Reality exists independently
- Fact is claim about Reality
- Knowledge is belief held by Knower
- Information is structured Knowledge for communication
- Representation portrays Information
- Carrier holds Representation

---

- Publication is Information structured for distribution
- Publication appears AFTER Knowledge, BEFORE Representation

**Evidence:**
