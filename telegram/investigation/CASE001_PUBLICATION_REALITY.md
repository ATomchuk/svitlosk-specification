# Section A — Reality

## Question 1: Does Publication exist outside SvitloSk?

**Answer:** PARTIALLY

Evidence:

- The word "publication" exists in common language (publishing industry, journalism, academia)
- The concept of "publishing information" exists independently of SvitloSk
- However, the specific SvitloSk Publication — as a structured, deterministic, territorial information unit — is an SvitloSk-specific abstraction
- The Telegram Glossary defines Publication as "one independent publication belonging to an Issue" — this specific meaning is SvitloSk-internal
- The GLOSSARY defines it as "a public information message generated by the SvitloSk system" — explicitly tying it to the system

**Sources:** GLOSSARY.md, TJS-000A

---

## Question 2: If SvitloSk disappeared tomorrow, would Publication still exist?

**Answer:** YES, but not in the SvitloSk sense

Evidence:

- The general concept of "publishing information for public consumption" would still exist
- Telegram channels would still exist
- Information about power outages would still be published by the DSO
- However, the specific SvitloSk Publication — with its deterministic rendering, territorial grammar, lifecycle states, and editorial constraints — would cease to exist
- The Telegram Journal would cease to exist
- The specific structured format would cease to exist

**Sources:** CHARTER.md, TJS-000, TJS-010

---

## Question 3: Is Publication a real-world object or an internal abstraction?

**Answer:** INTERNAL ABSTRACTION

Evidence:

- GLOSSARY.md: "A public information message generated by the SvitloSk system" — generated by the system, not found in nature
- DATA_MODEL.md: "Logical representation of interpreted information prepared for public distribution" — explicitly a logical representation
- TJS-000A: "One independent publication belonging to an Issue" — belongs to an SvitloSk-internal concept (Issue)
- TJS-010: Publication is "transformed" from normalized dataset — it is a product of processing
- No human can point to a physical "Publication" in the real world — they can point to a Telegram message, a printed schedule, a notification, but not to the abstract "Publication" entity

**Sources:** GLOSSARY.md, DATA_MODEL.md, TJS-000A, TJS-010

---

## Question 4: Who creates Publication?

**Answer:** MULTIPLE CREATORS

Evidence:

- GLOSSARY.md: "Publisher — The logical Role responsible for preparing and distributing Publications"
- TJS-010 §4.1: "Publication Engine — Transforms normalized dataset into deterministic Publication Package"
- TJS-010 §4.4: "Telegram Publisher — Telegram-specific implementation of Publisher Role"
- TJS-010 §5.1: "Administrators → Telegram Channel: Manual Publication" — Administrators also create Publications
- TJS-000A: "Manual Publications created by administrators" — explicitly different creator
- Legacy TJS-008 §13: "Manual Publications created by administrators SHALL remain outside the scope of automatic lifecycle management"

**Sources:** GLOSSARY.md, TJS-010, TJS-000A, Legacy TJS-008

---

## Question 5: Who consumes Publication?

**Answer:** MULTIPLE CONSUMERS

Evidence:

- GLOSSARY.md: "Information Consumer — Any person or software system receiving Publications generated by SvitloSk"
- TJS-010 §3.3: "Telegram Channel → Subscribers: Publication"
- TJS-000A: "Subscribers receive Publication"
- CHARTER.md: "residents of the Starokostiantyniv Community"
- SYSTEM_OVERVIEW.md: "residents, institutions and other community stakeholders"

**Sources:** GLOSSARY.md, TJS-010, TJS-000A, CHARTER.md, SYSTEM_OVERVIEW.md

## Question 6: Who owns Publication?

**Answer:** COMPLEX OWNERSHIP MODEL

Evidence:

- TJS-010 §4.1: Publication Engine "Owns: Publication generation, distribution, ownership model"
- Legacy TJS-007 §10: "Every Publication created by the Publisher becomes owned by the Publisher. Ownership SHALL be determined by the stored Telegram Message ID."
- TJS-010 §5.1: "Publication Engine → Telegram Channel: Publication (owned)"
- TJS-010 §5.2: "Publication Engine → Other publisher's Publications: ILLEGAL"
- TJS-021 §3.1: "Repository SHALL be the single authoritative source of truth for the publication state"
- Legacy TJS-007 §11: "Manual Publications SHALL never become owned Publications"

**Sources:** TJS-010, Legacy TJS-007, TJS-021

---

## Question 7: Can Publication exist without a recipient?

**Answer:** UNCLEAR

Evidence:

- GLOSSARY.md defines Publication as "for distribution through one or more supported communication channels" — implies distribution is essential
- TJS-021 §4.1: "A Generated Publication exists in the Repository but is not yet visible to readers" — implies it exists before recipients see it
- TJS-010 §3.3: Data flow ends at "Subscribers" — implies recipient is the end state
- However, Generated state exists before delivery — suggesting Publication can exist without recipient at that moment
- The Repository Authority Principle suggests the Repository is the truth, not the Telegram channel or the readers

**Sources:** GLOSSARY.md, TJS-021, TJS-010

---

## Question 8: Can Publication exist without Telegram?

**Answer:** YES

Evidence:

- TJS-000 §3: "Telegram itself SHALL NOT be considered the Journal. Telegram SHALL only be considered the publication platform."
- TJS-000 §3: "The Journal exists independently from any publication platform."
- GLOSSARY.md: "Publication Channel — A communication medium through which Publications are distributed. Examples include: Telegram Journal; future mobile application; future web interface."
- CHARTER.md: "SvitloSk is not defined by a particular technology, platform or communication channel."
- ARCHITECTURE.md: "Additional publication channels MAY be introduced without changing the architectural principles."

**Sources:** TJS-000, GLOSSARY.md, CHARTER.md, ARCHITECTURE.md
