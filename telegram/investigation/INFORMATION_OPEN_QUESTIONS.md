# Open Questions

## Question 1: What Is the Precise Definition of Information?

**Question:** What is the single, minimal, irreducible definition of Information?

**Current State:** Multiple definitions exist (property, relation, object, event, interpretation, representation, abstraction).

**Impact:** HIGH — affects ontological clarity.

**Evidence:**

- GLOSSARY.md uses "information" in multiple contexts
- No single definition provided
- Different documents imply different meanings

---

## Question 2: Can Information Exist Without a Knower?

**Question:** Does Information require a conscious observer, or can it exist independently?

**Current State:** Investigation suggests Information requires a Knower, but this is debated.

**Impact:** HIGH — affects fundamental ontology.

**Evidence:**

- Information requires Observation
- Observation requires a Knower
- But "information" can also mean "data" (which exists without knower)

---

## Question 3: What Is the Relationship Between Information and Data?

**Question:** Is Data a type of Information, or is Information a type of Data?

**Current State:** GLOSSARY.md distinguishes between "Open Data" and "information" but does not define the relationship.

**Impact:** MEDIUM — affects data model.

**Evidence:**

- GLOSSARY.md: "Open Data — Officially published information"
- GLOSSARY.md: "Data Model — The structured internal representation of Parsed Data"
- Relationship unclear

---

## Question 4: Can Information Be Created or Only Discovered?

**Question:** Does Observation CREATE Information, or does it DISCOVER Information that already exists?

**Current State:** Investigation suggests Observation creates Information, but this is debated.

**Impact:** MEDIUM — affects creation model.

**Evidence:**

- Before observation: no Knowledge, no Information
- After observation: Knowledge exists, Information exists
- But was Information "always there" waiting to be discovered?

---

## Question 5: What Happens to Information When It Is Forgotten?

**Question:** If no one remembers Information, does it still exist?

**Current State:** No evidence addresses this question.

**Impact:** LOW — affects persistence model.

**Evidence:**

- Information requires a Knower
- If all Knowers forget, does Information disappear?
- Or does Information persist in artifacts (books, databases)?

---

## Question 6: Is There a Difference Between Information and Meaning?

**Question:** Are Information and Meaning the same thing, or different?

**Current State:** GLOSSARY.md uses "meaning" and "information" separately but does not distinguish them.

**Impact:** MEDIUM — affects semantic model.

**Evidence:**

- GLOSSARY.md: "preserve the factual meaning of Open Data"
- GLOSSARY.md: "transforming structured information"
- Relationship unclear

---

## Question 7: Can Information Be Quantified?

**Question:** Can we measure Information (as Shannon suggests)?

**Current State:** SvitloSk does not quantify Information.

**Impact:** LOW — affects measurement model.

**Evidence:**

- Shannon's theory quantifies Information
- SvitloSk does not measure Information
- Relationship unclear

---

## Question 8: What Is the Relationship Between Information and Communication?

**Question:** Is Information the same as Communication, or different?

**Current State:** GLOSSARY.md distinguishes between "information" and "communication channels" but does not define the relationship.

**Impact:** LOW — affects communication model.

**Evidence:**

- GLOSSARY.md: "distribution through supported communication channels"
- Information is CONTENT; Communication is MECHANISM
- Relationship unclear
