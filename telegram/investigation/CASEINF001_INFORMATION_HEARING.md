## What Exists Before Any Observation?

**Answer:** Reality exists before observation.

**Evidence:**

- Power outage occurs whether or not anyone observes it
- Electricity availability is a physical state regardless of observation
- Addresses exist as physical locations regardless of observation

**Conclusion:** Reality is INDEPENDENT of observation.

# Part II: Observation

## What Changes When Reality Is Observed?

**Answer:** Knowledge changes.

**Evidence:**

- Before observation: Dispatcher does not know about outage
- After observation: Dispatcher knows about outage
- Reality does not change — only knowledge changes

**Conclusion:** Observation changes KNOWLEDGE, not REALITY.

---

## Is Observation Itself Information?

**Answer:** NO

**Evidence:**

- Observation is a PROCESS (act of observing)
- Information is the RESULT of observation
- Process and result are different things

**Conclusion:** Observation is a PROCESS that PRODUCES Information.

---

## Can Information Exist Without Observation?

**Answer:** UNCLEAR

**Evidence FOR:**

- Physical states exist without observation (electricity availability)
- But these are REALITY, not INFORMATION

**Evidence AGAINST:**

- Information requires a KNOWER
- Without observation, there is no knowledge
- Without knowledge, there is no information

**Conclusion:** Information REQUIRES observation to exist.

---

# Part III: Information Hypotheses

## Model A: Information is a Property of Reality

**Definition:** Information is an attribute that Reality possesses.

**Evidence:**

- Reality has properties (electricity available/unavailable)
- These properties can be described
- Description is information

**Fit:** PARTIAL — explains factual information but not interpreted information.

---

## Model B: Information is a Relation

**Definition:** Information is a relationship between Reality and a Knower.

**Evidence:**

- Information connects Reality to Knowledge
- Information requires both source and receiver
- Information is about something

**Fit:** GOOD — explains why information requires observation.

---

## Model C: Information is an Object

**Definition:** Information is a thing that can be created, stored, transmitted.

**Evidence:**

- Information can be written down
- Information can be transmitted
- Information can be archived

**Fit:** PARTIAL — explains information as artifact but not as concept.

---

## Model D: Information is an Event

**Definition:** Information is something that happens when Reality is observed.

**Evidence:**

- Information appears when observation occurs
- Information can be created and destroyed
- Information has temporal dimension

**Fit:** PARTIAL — explains information creation but not persistence.

---

## Model E: Information is a Human Interpretation

**Definition:** Information is how humans understand Reality.

**Evidence:**

- Information requires interpretation
- Different people may interpret differently
- Information is subjective

**Fit:** PARTIAL — explains subjective information but not objective facts.

---

## Model F: Information is a Representation

**Definition:** Information is a representation of Reality for a Knower.

**Evidence:**

- Information represents Reality
- Information is not Reality itself
- Information can be accurate or inaccurate

**Fit:** GOOD — explains why information can be wrong.

---

## Model G: Information is an Abstraction

**Definition:** Information is an abstract concept derived from Reality.

**Evidence:**

- Information is not physical
- Information can be shared
- Information can be copied without loss

**Fit:** PARTIAL — explains information properties but not origin.

# Part VI: Official Publication

## Does Official Publication CREATE Information?

**Answer:** NO

**Evidence:**

- Information already exists (dispatcher knows)
- Official Publication DISCLOSES information to public
- Official Publication makes private information public

**Conclusion:** Official Publication DISCLOSES, not CREATES.

---

## Does Official Publication CHANGE Information?

**Answer:** NO

**Evidence:**

- GLOSSARY.md: "SvitloSk SHALL NOT modify the factual meaning of Open Data"
- Official Publication preserves factual meaning
- Official Publication only changes ACCESS LEVEL (private → public)

**Conclusion:** Official Publication changes ACCESS, not MEANING.

---

# Part VII: Open Data Ontology

## Is Open Data Information?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- GLOSSARY.md: "Open Data — Officially published information"
- Open Data IS information that is publicly accessible
- Open Data has all properties of Information

**Conclusion:** Open Data IS Information.

---

## Is Open Data a Representation?

**Answer:** PARTIALLY

**Evidence:**

- Open Data represents Reality for the public
- Open Data is not Reality itself
- Open Data can be accurate or inaccurate

**Conclusion:** Open Data is Information that REPRESENTS Reality.

---

## Is Open Data a Container?

**Answer:** NO

**Evidence:**

- Open Data is the information itself
- Open Data is not a vessel containing information
- Open Data IS the content

**Conclusion:** Open Data is NOT a container.

---

## Is Open Data Evidence?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Open Data provides evidence of Reality
- Open Data can be used to verify facts
- Open Data is traceable to source

**Conclusion:** Open Data IS evidence of Reality.

---

## Is Open Data a Projection?

**Answer:** PARTIALLY

**Evidence:**

- Open Data projects Reality for public consumption
- Open Data is one representation of Reality
- Open Data may omit details

**Conclusion:** Open Data IS a projection of Reality.

---

# Part VIII: Where Publication Begins

## Does Publication Create Information?

**Answer:** NO

**Evidence:**

- GLOSSARY.md: "A Publication SHALL NOT introduce new facts"
- Publication preserves factual meaning
- Publication does not create information

**Conclusion:** Publication does NOT create Information.

---

## Does Publication Package Information?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Publication packages Interpretation Result
- Publication structures information for delivery
- Publication organizes information for readability

**Conclusion:** Publication PACKAGES Information.

---

## Does Publication Distribute Information?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Publication is distributed through Publication Channels
- Publication reaches subscribers
- Publication delivers information to residents

**Conclusion:** Publication DISTRIBUTES Information.

# Part IX: Collapse Experiments

## Experiment 1: Remove Information

**Question:** Can Publication exist?

**Answer:** NO

**Evidence:**

- Publication is "public information message"
- Without Information, Publication has no content
- Publication cannot exist without Information

**Verdict:** Information is ESSENTIAL.

---

## Experiment 2: Remove Official Publication

**Question:** Can Information exist?

**Answer:** YES

**Evidence:**

- Information exists before Official Publication (dispatcher knows)
- Official Publication only makes Information public
- Information exists independently of Official Publication

**Verdict:** Official Publication is NOT essential for Information existence.

---

## Experiment 3: Remove Observation

**Question:** Can Information exist?

**Answer:** NO

**Evidence:**

- Information requires a Knower
- Observation creates Knowledge
- Without Observation, no Knowledge, no Information

**Verdict:** Observation is ESSENTIAL for Information.

---

## Experiment 4: Remove Reality

**Question:** Can Information exist?

**Answer:** NO

**Evidence:**

- Information is about Reality
- Without Reality, there is nothing to be informed about
- Information requires a subject (Reality)

**Verdict:** Reality is ESSENTIAL for Information.

# Part XI: Philosophical Comparison

## Claude Shannon

**Shannon's view:** Information is a measure of uncertainty reduction.

**SvitloSk fit:** PARTIAL — SvitloSk reduces uncertainty about outages, but Information is more than uncertainty reduction.

---

## Gregory Bateson

**Bateson's view:** Information is "a difference that makes a difference."

**SvitloSk fit:** GOOD — SvitloSk's Information makes a difference to residents (they know about outages).

---

## Karl Popper (World 1/2/3)

**Popper's view:**

- World 1: Physical reality
- World 2: Mental reality (knowledge)
- World 3: Objective knowledge (information)

**SvitloSk fit:** GOOD — SvitloSk operates in World 3 (objective information about outages).

---

## Michael Buckland

**Buckland's view:** Information can be: (1) knowledge, (2) thing, (3) process.

**SvitloSk fit:** GOOD — SvitloSk's Information is: (1) Knowledge about outages, (2) Publication as thing, (3) Processing as process.

---

## DIKW Hierarchy

**DIKW:** Data → Information → Knowledge → Wisdom

**SvitloSk fit:** PARTIAL — SvitloSk goes from Data to Information, but not to Knowledge or Wisdom.

---

## Domain-Driven Design

**DDD view:** Information is part of the domain model.

**SvitloSk fit:** GOOD — Information is a core domain concept in SvitloSk.
