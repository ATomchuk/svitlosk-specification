# Open Questions

## Question 1: Is Publication Purely Information Object?

**Question:** Is Publication ONLY an Information Object, or does it have other nature?

**Current State:** Publication is classified as Information Object created for Communication purpose.

**Impact:** HIGH — affects ontological clarity.

**Evidence:**

- Publication has identity, lifecycle, ownership
- Publication is more than Information
- But Publication is not pure Communication

---

## Question 2: Can Publication Exist Without Communication Purpose?

**Question:** Would Publication still exist if it had no Communication purpose?

**Current State:** Publication's purpose is Communication; unclear if it could exist without purpose.

**Impact:** MEDIUM — affects purpose model.

**Evidence:**

- Publication is created specifically for distribution
- Without distribution purpose, Publication loses reason to exist
- But Publication could exist as artifact

---

## Question 3: What Is the Precise Boundary Between Publication and Representation?

**Question:** Where exactly does Publication end and Representation begin?

**Current State:** Publication is content; Representation is form. But boundary is unclear.

**Impact:** MEDIUM — affects boundary clarity.

**Evidence:**

- Publication is created from Interpretation Result
- Publication is rendered as Telegram Message
- Boundary is at rendering step

---

## Question 4: Does Communication Require Publication?

**Question:** Can Communication occur without Publication?

**Current State:** SvitloSk's Communication requires Publication; but Communication in general does not.

**Impact:** LOW — affects scope definition.

**Evidence:**

- People can communicate without formal Publication
- But SvitloSk's specific Communication requires Publication
- Scope matters

---

## Question 5: What Is the Relationship Between Publication and Editorial Object?

**Question:** Is Publication an Editorial Object or something else?

**Current State:** Publication follows Editorial Policy; but Publication is more than editorial.

**Impact:** LOW — affects classification.

**Evidence:**

- TJS-020: Editorial System governs Publications
- But Publication is also Information Object, Communication Object
- Multiple classifications possible

---

## Question 6: Can Publication Be Both Information and Communication?

**Question:** Can Publication be classified as both Information and Communication?

**Current State:** Publication is Information Object created for Communication purpose.

**Impact:** LOW — affects model complexity.

**Evidence:**

- Publication contains Information
- Publication is used for Communication
- But Publication is not Communication itself

---

## Question 7: What Happens to Publication After Communication?

**Question:** Does Publication cease to exist after Communication, or persist?

**Current State:** Publication persists as historical record.

**Impact:** LOW — affects lifecycle model.

**Evidence:**

- TJS-021 §4.4: "Archived Publications remain available permanently"
- Publication persists after Communication
- But Publication's purpose is fulfilled

---

## Question 8: Is Publication a Boundary or a Bridge?

**Question:** Is Publication a boundary between concepts or a bridge connecting them?

**Current State:** Publication bridges Information and Representation.

**Impact:** LOW — affects metaphor choice.

**Evidence:**

- Publication connects Information to Representation
- Publication is the transition point
- Both "boundary" and "bridge" are valid metaphors
