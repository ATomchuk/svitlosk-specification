# 1. Translation Policy Decisions

## 1.1 Document IDs

| Decision | Statement | Justification |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Document IDs remain in English | Document IDs are permanent identifiers, not human-readable content | International standard; IDs are language-independent |

## 1.2 Status Values

| Decision | Statement | Justification |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Status values remain in English | Status is an architectural classification, not content | International standard; status values are language-independent |

## 1.3 RFC 2119 Keywords

| Decision | Statement | Justification |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| RFC 2119 keywords remain in English | SHALL, SHOULD, MAY are international standards | RFC 2119 is an IETF standard; translating would break normative compliance |

## 1.4 Section Numbering

| Decision | Statement | Justification |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Section numbering remains identical | Section numbers are language-independent | Translation SHALL preserve structure |

## 1.5 Document Titles

| Decision | Statement | Justification |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Document titles are translated | Titles are human-readable | Ukrainian readers need to understand document purpose |

## 1.6 Section Titles

| Decision | Statement | Justification |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Section titles are translated | Section titles are human-readable | Ukrainian readers need to understand section purpose |

## 1.7 Content

| Decision | Statement | Justification |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Technical content is translated | Content is human-readable | Ukrainian readers need to understand specifications |
| Examples are translated | Examples are human-readable | Ukrainian readers need to understand examples |
